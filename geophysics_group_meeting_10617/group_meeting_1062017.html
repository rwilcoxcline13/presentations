Moments

  • Seismic Moment (Mo): Measures overall source deformation
    • Related to global plate motion, polar motion, and Earth's rotation.

  • Surface Wave Magnitude (Ms): Measured using the largest amplitude

  • Moment Magnitude (Mw): Magnitude that is directly tied to earthquake source processes

Stress Drop

  • Stress drop: the magnitude of stress released by an earthquake
    • Complete Stress Drop: Final stress is equal to the dynamic frictional stress (Orowans Hypothesis)
    • Partial Stress Drop: Final stress is greater than frictional stress

  • Relates slip, fault dimensions, and seismic moment



Relationship between fault area and the seismic moment. Solid lines indicate lines of constant stress drop.

Energy Release

  • How is wave energy related to moment and magnitude?
    • Reflective of different fault environments?
    • Release all stress or only part of it?

  • Depends on complete or partial stress drop
    • Complete stress drop → Strain Release = Wave Energy
    • Partial stress drop → Lower bound on wave energy
